Browse Movements: Positions

First Position

Plié is the most important movement in ballet.
We start every class with a plié combination.
Keep your back straight and push your body down between your legs.
Bend your knees to the side.
Keep both your heels on the floor. Keep your weight on your small toes and heels.
Don’t let the arches of your feet fall.

Second Position

Tendu is the second movement at the barre.
It can be performed to the front, side or back.
Slide your foot against the floor.
Bring your heels up to a demi point.
Then point your toes on the floor. Keep both knees tight.
Put your weight on your small toes and heels of the standing leg
Don’t let the arches of your feet fall.

Third Position

Start with a tendu
Once your leg is fully extended lift it off the floor.
Place is back on the floor.
And close.

Put your weight on your small toes and heels of the standing leg
Don’t let the arches of your feet fall.

Fourth Position

This movement can be performed either En Dedans (inside or anti-clockwise) or En Dehors (outside or clockwise). Start with a Tendu. Once your leg is extended rotate it around to the other side. This video shows only En Dehors.

Fondu

This movement can be performed either to the front, side or back. Start with both legs straight. Slowly bend both legs while lifting one in a coup de pie above the floor.
